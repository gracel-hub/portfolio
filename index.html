About

I grew up in Jackson Heights, Queens and started my career at Merrill Lynch and BNP Paribas in New York, in interest rate derivatives trading operations and later institutional sales covering fixed income, credit derivatives, and mortgage-backed securities. A few years in, I moved to San Francisco when BNP transferred me out West.

When that chapter closed, I took time off to backpack through South America. It was one of the best decisions I've made. After that I joined early-stage fintechs in the Bay Area. At Wealthfront, I helped build the foundational service and brokerage operations functions for an automated investment platform, including the operational systems, workflows, and internal tools that supported new product rollouts as AUM scaled from $2B to $20B. At Navan, I worked on operations for an automated expense management platform that connected corporate card transactions with travel policies in real time. Both companies went public in 2025.

I tend to be the person who figures out how things actually work. Translating between engineers and compliance teams, designing processes that hold everything together, noticing the gaps before they turn into problems. I went back to school to earn an MSc in Sustainability Management at Columbia because I wanted to bring that same energy to climate and energy work. I spent several years consulting on GHG inventories, GRI reporting, and a biodiversity risk framework for ING Group's lending team. Now I'm at Columbia's MS Climate Finance program, helping the next wave of practitioners find their footing in a field that doesn't always make itself easy to navigate.

Outside of work, I've studied abroad in New Zealand (where I fell in love with the outdoors), completed a half-Ironman and multiple marathons, and spend as much time as possible hiking, camping, and bike touring. I also really love a good jigsaw puzzle and cooking for people I care about.
